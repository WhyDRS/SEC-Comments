_Worknig notesss_

1. **NASD was censured as an SRO.**
   SEC found NASD failed its Exchange Act duties as a self-regulatory organization, especially under Sections 19(g) and 19(h). 

2. **Nasdaq market makers had serious anticompetitive problems.**
   The order describes artificially inflexible spreads, coordinated quotes/trades/trade reports, retaliation against competitive quoting, and failures to honor quotes or report trades properly. 

3. **NASD knew enough by 1990 and did not act adequately.**
   SEC said NASD had information showing possible misconduct, including even-eighth quoting conventions and retaliation against firms narrowing spreads, but failed to investigate and enforce effectively. 

4. **Regulatory capture / undue market-maker influence is the core governance point.**
   SEC tied NASD’s regulatory failures partly to “undue influence of Nasdaq market makers in the regulatory processes of the NASD.” That is probably the strongest cite for SRO design needing insulation from commercial member interests. 

5. **The remedial structure is the useful model.**
   SEC required: at least 50% independent public/non-industry governance; autonomous regulatory staff; consistent access standards; internal audit reporting to independent/public governors; professional hearing officers; and independent consultant review. 

6. **Audit trail mandate is especially relevant to CAT / market reconstruction.**
   NASD had to build an audit trail sufficient to reconstruct markets, surveil effectively, enforce rules, record orders from first customer/counterparty contact through execution or non-execution, and synchronize clocks marketwide. 

7. **Trade reporting and firm quote enforcement were explicit failures.**
   SEC specifically found failures to enforce the firm quote rule and trade reporting obligations, then ordered improvements in trade reporting reliability and firm quote enforcement. 

Best-use framing: this is a **regulatory-capture precedent for why SROs need independent governance, staff autonomy, uniform access standards, independent audit, and auditable market reconstruction systems**. For your purposes, the cleanest bridge is probably: *if an SRO’s commercial participants can shape regulation, access, surveillance, or enforcement, the system predictably fails in exactly the way the SEC already documented in NASD/Nasdaq.*
