## Procedural Posture

This was not a full merits amicus brief. It was a stay/injunction brief. The filing supported ASA and Citadel's attempt to stop CAT fee collection while the court reviewed the 2023 funding order.

## Core Positions

1. The CAT funding order shifted CAT costs disproportionately to broker-dealers.

Virtu/SIFMA argue the SEC-approved 2023 model assigned two-thirds of CAT costs to broker-dealers and exposed them to further SRO pass-through costs.

Key references:

- Majority of CAT costs shifted to broker-dealers: `amicus-briefs/135 - virtu-financial-inc-service-date-12-02-2024-email-atto.md:460`
- Two-thirds allocation to broker-dealers: `amicus-briefs/135 - virtu-financial-inc-service-date-12-02-2024-email-atto.md:462`
- SROs sought to shift all CAT-related costs onto broker-dealer members: `amicus-briefs/135 - virtu-financial-inc-service-date-12-02-2024-email-atto.md:540`

2. Fee collection before judicial review would undermine the petition.

Virtu/SIFMA argue that SRO rule filings would let CAT fees be collected before the Eleventh Circuit decided whether the SEC funding order was lawful.

Key references:

- SROs attempting to collect while review pending: `amicus-briefs/135 - virtu-financial-inc-service-date-12-02-2024-email-atto.md:470`
- Broker-dealers face an impossible position absent suspension: `amicus-briefs/135 - virtu-financial-inc-service-date-12-02-2024-email-atto.md:482`
- Stay needed to protect effectiveness of the petition: `amicus-briefs/135 - virtu-financial-inc-service-date-12-02-2024-email-atto.md:490`

3. Broker-dealers would suffer irreparable harm because fees may be unrecoverable.

The brief's strongest practical argument is that broker-dealers could pay CAT fees and then have no practical recovery later because of nonrefundability and SRO immunity.

Key references:

- Irreparable harm to industry members: `amicus-briefs/135 - virtu-financial-inc-service-date-12-02-2024-email-atto.md:492`
- Broker-dealers and investors forced to pay: `amicus-briefs/135 - virtu-financial-inc-service-date-12-02-2024-email-atto.md:571`
- No requirement to return funds even if the order is unlawful: `amicus-briefs/135 - virtu-financial-inc-service-date-12-02-2024-email-atto.md:588`
- Nonrefundable CAT reporter payments: `amicus-briefs/135 - virtu-financial-inc-service-date-12-02-2024-email-atto.md:598`
- SRO immunity problem: `amicus-briefs/135 - virtu-financial-inc-service-date-12-02-2024-email-atto.md:630`

4. A stay would preserve the status quo without harming SEC enforcement.

Virtu/SIFMA argue a stay would not interfere with SEC regulatory objectives, because it would simply prevent unrecoverable payments while review proceeded.

## Comment Use

Virtu is the clean market-maker example outside Citadel. Its brief helps show that at least one other major market maker participated in the CAT funding litigation as an amicus, and that its emphasis was practical: preventing broker-dealers from paying allegedly unlawful, nonrefundable CAT fees before judicial review could mean anything.
