# 24-12300 High-Value Filing Analysis

Use this file for the related Citadel CAT petition that is mostly procedural and held in abeyance.

## `1 - agency-case-docketed-petition-for-review/1-2 - agency-case-docketed-petition-for-review.md`

- `claim`: Citadel petitioned for review of a May 2024 SEC order granting temporary conditional exemptive relief from CAT NMS Plan requirements. The challenged order concerned CAT reporting obligations and exemptions, not the 2023 CAT funding order itself.
- `who benefits`: Citadel benefits from preserving appellate review of CAT-related exemptions. SEC, CAT LLC, and SROs benefit from the exemptive relief because it gives CAT reporters and CAT operations more time to implement or adapt to reporting changes.
- `money fact`: This petition is not primarily a fee case. Its money relevance is indirect: it sits in the same CAT litigation portfolio as the funding challenges and can be used to show repeated pressure on CAT implementation.
- `surveillance/privacy fact`: The exemptive order relates to the scope and timing of CAT reporting obligations. It is useful for showing that CAT disputes are not limited to fee allocation; they also involve what must be reported into the audit trail and when.
- `governance fact`: The petition challenges SEC use of exemptive authority over the CAT NMS Plan. The relevant governance issue is the SEC's ability to adjust CAT implementation requirements through orders and exemptions while SROs and CAT LLC administer the system.
- `court holding`: No merits holding in the petition itself.
- `comment use`: Use as background evidence that Citadel's CAT strategy is broader than a single funding order. Do not treat this case as a merits ruling against CAT.

## `42 - motion-to-hold-appeal-in-abeyance-filed-by-citadel-motion-is-unopposed-42-2.md`

- `claim`: Citadel asked the Eleventh Circuit to keep the petition in abeyance because the SEC announced a comprehensive review of CAT that could materially affect or moot the petition. Intervenors did not oppose.
- `who benefits`: Citadel benefits by preserving the petition without spending briefing resources while the SEC reconsiders CAT. SEC, CAT LLC, and intervenors benefit because abeyance avoids immediate merits litigation during the comprehensive review.
- `money fact`: The motion ties the abeyance request to broader CAT issues, including funding changes after the 23-13396 vacatur, but the motion itself does not seek fee relief.
- `surveillance/privacy fact`: The motion cites the SEC's comprehensive review as potentially covering CAT design, functionality, and the scope of collected information, including recent approval of changes ending collection of some personal identifying information.
- `governance fact`: The abeyance request confirms that SEC-led comprehensive review is becoming the central governance venue for CAT issues, including implementation, data scope, cost allocation, and reserve/funding questions.
- `court holding`: The motion asks for abeyance; it is not a merits holding. The docket reflects the court kept the matter paused with periodic status reporting.
- `comment use`: Use to connect the concept-release comment to live litigation posture: parties themselves are telling the court that the SEC's comprehensive CAT review may change the need for adjudication.

- `claim`: The status report states that the case should remain in abeyance because the SEC's comprehensive CAT review remains ongoing and because the new 2026 funding-order challenge, 26-10936, could affect whether this petition needs adjudication.
- `who benefits`: Citadel benefits from maintaining optionality across related CAT challenges. SEC, CAT LLC, and intervenors benefit from avoiding piecemeal adjudication during the SEC review.
- `money fact`: The report expressly links this procedural case to the March 2026 CAT funding order, which funds CAT through March 2028 while the SEC conducts its review.
- `surveillance/privacy fact`: The report notes the concept release and public comment process, including the June 22, 2026 comment deadline, as part of the SEC's comprehensive CAT review.
- `governance fact`: The filing shows live coordination among appellate litigation, SEC rulemaking/concept-release work, and CAT funding orders.
- `court holding`: No merits holding. It is a status filing supporting continued abeyance.
- `comment use`: Use to show that the concept-release period is not academic. The parties are invoking it in court as a reason to pause litigation and wait for the SEC's CAT review.
