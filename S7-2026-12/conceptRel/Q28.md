
For a Central Time → Eastern Time conversion mismatch, the "correct data" is not guessed from CAT. You reconstruct it from the underlying event sources, then use CAT linkage to prove which side is wrong.

CAT requires reportable events with timestamps, and the SEC's Rule 613 framework requires synchronized business clocks and timestamps in millisecond-or-finer increments. CAT's FAQ says STRING timestamps must be reported in Eastern Time, while UTC timestamps must not be adjusted to Eastern.
